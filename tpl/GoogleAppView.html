FAQ’s:

Why use email protector?

When we send an email, we consider it pretty safe, right?


Well, consider this.. Your email can at any time be read by:


1. A hacker who gets access to your mailbox

2. A hacker who gets into your recipients' mailboxes

3. An unintended party because your email was forwarded

4. An unintended party because your email was copied and pasted

5. A government organization that subpoenas the recipient

6. Someone who gets access to your or your recipient’s hard drives or storage devices

7. Any of the extensions that are in your browser

8. Any service that you’ve allowed to access your email and calendar


By using something like an email protector that encrypts the content of your email, you insure that no matter who gets or finds your communication will not be able to open it without having a proper key or authenticating themselves to be the person you’ve meant to see it.


How does Email Protector Work?

Biomio email protector works by encrypting the contents and attachments of your email using PGP encryption (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pretty_Good_Privacy). While the concept of PGP encryption is not new, what Biomio does it makes the process of encryption and decryption very easy and natural. You and the recipient don’t have to worry about the key exchange (we handle all that for you behind the scenes) and authentication happens seamlessly by the recipient pressing their finger on the fingerprint sensor or holding up the camera for face recognition.

What happens when I send an email to someone who’s not registered on Biomio?

If you send an email to someone who’s not registered on Biomio, when they receive your message, it will begin with a line that prompts them to register an account:



Once they register an account, they will be able to decrypt the content of your email.


Will the recipient of the email be able to forward it to someone else?

The recipient will be able to forward your encrypted email to someone else, however the other party will not be able to decrypt it if you haven’t authorized them to do so.


What if the recipient or I don’t use Gmail, can I still use Biomio Email Protector?

We are working on supporting other email clients, but for now you can most likely still make it work by using Gmail as your email client connecting to your current email server as a POP3 account.

Am I in danger by registering my biometrics with Biomio?

Biomio is a company that has developed an engine for risk-free password-less authentication. This includes client and server-side biometrics. Client-side biometrics is something we’re grown accustomed to with Apple’s touch ID and other mobile phone solutions, where the biometric data never leaves your device. Server-side biometrics (like face recognition does require server-side processing, however the data is encrypted and stored as s series of points of interest while the original images are thrown out. This approach is very safe as it goes to the great length to protect your biometric data (if any of it is collected).
